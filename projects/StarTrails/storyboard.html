Storyboard (Low-fidelity prototype)

Using the information we gathered from our users and the resulting list of high-priority functional requirements we subsequently indentified, we then set our to create a low-fidelity prototype. Using pen and paper, our team created different sketches on what we as a development team believe our app should look like. The Low-Fidelity Prototyping phase is incredibly useful for our development cycle, as it allows us to brainstorm different conceptual ideas we may have onto paper and gather feedback on which design aspects may work the best for our application. Although this level of prototyping is useful for early user interface brainstorming, we are constrained on basic interface functionality and general operating systems look and feel.

We decided that the first design (pictured on the upper left) had all of the aspects that we wanted to include in our prototype. We set out to follow that design, and determine later on whether or not we wanted to make changes to the initial sketches. This initial design had some more functionality that we wanted to see included, such as the navigation bar at the bottom and a better utilization of space from the event blocks. The bottom sketches are what the user would see when they clicked on eny given event. The event view would later house some of the most important information on the app. The main goal is to give users a place to find events, and we decided that we wanted to implement these early sketches into our app.

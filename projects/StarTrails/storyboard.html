Storyboard (Low-fidelity prototype)

Using the information we gathered from our users and the resulting list of high-priority functional requirements we subsequently indentified, we then set our to create a low-fidelity prototype. Using pen and paper, our team created different sketches on what we as a development team believe our app should look like. The Low-Fidelity Prototyping phase is incredibly useful for our development cycle, as it allows us to brainstorm different conceptual ideas we may have onto paper and gather feedback on which design aspects may work the best for our application. Although this level of prototyping is useful for early user interface brainstorming, we are constrained on basic interface functionality and general operating systems look and feel.

Individual Sketches

Below you'll find the individual annotated sketches of our low-fidelity prototype. They illustrate the start of what our app might look like and includes functionality that is described in our requirements specification documentation.

Above are the login/registration page since they are the easiest to create. It is no surprise that they are similar because it is pretty standard and everyone is probably used to them by now. However, you can already see the different styles that we had in mind.

Here is where our designs start to diverge. We have similar ideas but with different placements and styles for our elements. Here are a few examples of similarities:

  • A map view that shows all the stargazing locations centered on Montana
  • Buttons to denote overlays for the map
  • Bottom element for discovery of sites or navigation
  • Profile button to view personal information

We decided that the image on the left had great icons for overlay buttons. The overlays are useful for users to be able to locate the best locations to stargaze at a glance. For the image in the center we agreed on the navigation system at the bottom so users can navigate our site with ease. Finally, the last two images had less of a map view which allowed us to fit more content including the navigation system at the bottom. For the rest of our interface, we thought it was better to split it up into separate pages or moved to other pages.

Final Designs

Here are the final designs for our low-fidelity prototype which includes elements from all of our individual sketches. The biggest challenge was to figure out how to fit all our information on the pages we had or to create new pages. We have a lot of information available that we can provide and would be useful for most of our users, but we don't have as much space as we would like. Thankfully, we have a creative team and was able to add just about everything to our final design.
